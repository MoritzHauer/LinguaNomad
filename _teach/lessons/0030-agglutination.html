Lesson 0030

How Kyrgyz Words Are Built — Agglutination

Unlock the logic of Kyrgyz grammar. Every suffix adds exactly one meaning; the root never changes. Once you see this, any Kyrgyz word becomes readable.

Mission: grammar Skill: analysis Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0029 — At Work

What Is Agglutination?

Kyrgyz is an agglutinative language. That means meaning is built by stacking suffixes one after another onto a root stem. Each suffix adds exactly one grammatical idea — plural, possession, case, tense — and the root itself never changes shape.

This is very different from English, where words like go / went / gone change their root entirely. In Kyrgyz, the root stays put and you read the suffixes from left to right to decode the full meaning.

The key rule: to read an unknown Kyrgyz word, find the root at the left edge and strip suffixes from right to left.

Two Worked Examples

Example 1: бала (child) → балалардан

PieceWhat it adds
балаchild (root)
-ларplural → children
-ымmy (1st person possessive) → my children
-данfrom (ablative) → from my children

Full word: балалардан = "from my children"

Example 2: үй (house) → үйлөрдөгү

PieceWhat it adds
үйhouse (root)
-лөрplural → houses
-дөlocative (in/at) → in the houses
-гүrelativiser → that is in the houses / of the houses

Full word: үйлөрдөгү = "that is in the houses"

Key Suffix Catalogue

Suffix family What it adds
-лар/-лер/-дар/-дерplural
-ым/-им/-ум/-үмmy (1st person singular possessive)
-ыңыз/-иңизyour (2nd person formal possessive)
-да/-де/-та/-теlocative: in / at / on
