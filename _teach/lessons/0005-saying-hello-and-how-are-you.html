Lesson 0005

Saying Hello & How Are You

Three essential greeting exchanges that open every conversation in Kyrgyz — learn them in both directions so you can give and receive.

Mission: family Mission: travel Time: 10 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0004 — Reading Kyrgyz Words Aloud

Exchange 1 — Hello

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Салам! Salam! Hi! / Hello!
О, салам! O, salam! Oh, hi!

Салам is the everyday greeting between peers and friends. It covers "hi", "hello", and "hey". You respond with exactly the same word — or add a warm О, at the start.

Exchange 2 — How Are You?

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Кандайсыз? Kandaysyz? How are you?
Жакшы, рахмат. Өзүңүз? Zhakshy, rakhmat. Özüñüz? Fine, thanks. And you?

Кандайсыз is the slightly more formal "how are you" — safe to use with anyone. The response gives you two high-frequency words in one go: Жакшы (good/fine) and Рахмат (thank you).

Exchange 3 — How Are Things?

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Иштер кандай? Ishter kanday? How are things? / How's work?
Жакшы Zhakshy Fine / Good
Болуп жатат Bolup zhatat Going well / Getting along

Иштер кандай is a casual follow-up once you've said the first hello. Both responses are equally natural — Жакшы is quicker, Болуп жатат is a little warmer.

Сиз vs Сен — Which "You"?

Сиз is the formal, respectful "you" — use it with anyone you've just met, elders, and people in a position of authority. Сен is the informal "you" — for close friends, children, and peers you know well. When in doubt, default to Сиз. It is never wrong; it signals respect.
