Lesson 0039

Asking for Permission

Form a polite permission request for any verb using the -сам/-сем болобу? pattern, and understand the yes and no answers.

Mission: communication Skill: grammar Time: 15–20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0038 — Expressing Opinions

The Permission Pattern

One formula covers every permission request in Kyrgyz:

verb stem + -сам / -сем + болобу?

= May I [verb]?

Positive answer

Ооба, болот

Yes, you may / Yes, it is possible

Negative answer

Жок, болбойт

No, you may not / No, it is not possible

Examples

VerbPermission formEnglish
кир– (to enter) Кирсем болобу? May I come in?
ал– (to take) Алсам болобу? May I take it?
колдон– (to use) Колдонсом болобу? May I use it?
отур– (to sit) Отурсам болобу? May I sit down?
сүйлө– (to speak) Сүйлөсөм болобу? May I speak?
кет– (to go/leave) Кетсем болобу? May I go?

Vowel Harmony Guide

Which suffix you use — -сам or -сем/-сом/-сөм — depends on the last vowel in the verb stem.

Stem vowels (back)SuffixExample
а, о, у, ы -сам / -сом ал– → Алсам болобу?  |  колдон– → Колдонсом болобу?
Stem vowels (front)SuffixExample
э, ө, ү, и, е -сем / -сөм кет– → Кетсем болобу?  |  сүйлө– → Сүйлөсөм болобу?

Болот means "it is possible / it is allowed". Болбойт is its negative. Both come from болуу (to be / to become).

Cultural note: Permission as Good Upbringing

In Kyrgyz culture, asking permission — even for small things — signals respect and good upbringing (адептүүлүк). When visiting a Kyrgyz home, saying Кирсем болобу? (May I come in?) before entering a room is always appreciated. Hosts read this as a sign that you were raised well.
