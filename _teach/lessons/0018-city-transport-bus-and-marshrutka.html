Marshrutka Culture

A маршрутка (marshrutka) is a minibus van running a fixed route — the backbone of urban transport in Bishkek and other Kyrgyz cities. Here is what to expect:

  • Flag it down anywhere along the route — it will stop if there is room.
  • Pay directly — hand coins or notes to the driver or assistant when you board or alight; fares are typically 8–15 som.
  • Call your stop before you reach it: say your destination or Токтот! when you are close.
  • There are no electronic announcements — watch out the window and listen to other passengers.

Getting Off

Токтот!

Toktot! — Stop! (shout this to the driver)

Мен түшөм

Men tüshöm — I'm getting off

Quick Tips

  • Say Токтот! loudly — drivers will not notice a whisper.
  • Move toward the door before you call the stop.
  • On a crowded marshrutka, pass your fare forward through other passengers.
  • БАРАБЫ? (baraby?) turns any verb into a yes/no question.
