Lesson 0036

Likes and Dislikes

Say what you like and don't like, ask someone else, and react with enthusiasm or disappointment in Kyrgyz.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 15–20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0035 — Clarification

Key Phrases

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Мага … жагат Maga … jagat I like …
Мага … жакпайт Maga … jakpayt I don't like …
Сизге жагабы? Sizge jagabı? Do you like it?
Абдан жакшы! Abdan jakshy! Very good! / Excellent!
Сонун экен! Sonun eken! How wonderful! / Great!
Жаман эмес Jaman emes Not bad
Эч жакпайт Ech jakpayt I really don't like it

Grammar: The Дative of Pleasure

The Kyrgyz like/dislike structure is not "I like X" but "To me, X is pleasing." The verb жагат (is pleasing) is 3rd person singular and the experiencer takes the dative case.

KyrgyzLiteral glossNatural English
Мага чай жагат. To me tea is-pleasing. I like tea.
Мага эт жакпайт. To me meat is-not-pleasing. I don't like meat.
Сизге кымыз жагабы? To you kymyz is-pleasing-Q? Do you like kymyz?

Мага = dative of мен (I → to me). Сизге = dative of сиз (you → to you). Swap out any noun after the dative pronoun to talk about anything.

Enthusiasm & Reactions

Positive

Абдан жакшы! — Excellent! (general approval)

Сонун экен! — How wonderful! (discovery, pleasant surprise)

Мага абдан жагат! — I like it very much!

Neutral & Negative

Жаман эмес — Not bad (mild approval)

Эч жакпайт — I really don't like it (strong dislike)

Cultural note: Kymyz Etiquette

If offered kymyz (fermented mare's milk) and you don't like it, Жаман эмес (not bad) is a gracious middle path. Hosts take pride in their kymyz, so Эч жакпайт is honest but blunt — save it for contexts where directness is welcome.
