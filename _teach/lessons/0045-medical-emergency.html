Lesson 0045

Medical Emergency

You will be able to report an emergency, say where it hurts, and understand a basic instruction from a first responder.

Mission: survival Skill: conversation Time: 20–25 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0044 — Making a Phone Call

Emergency Phrases

These are the phrases you need to call for help and communicate your situation.

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Жардам!Jardam!Help!
Шашылыш жардам!Shashılış jardam!Emergency! / Call an ambulance!
Мага жардам керекMaga jardam kerekI need help
Дарыгер керекDarıger kerekI need a doctor
Ооруканага алып барыңызOorukanaga alıp barınızTake me to the hospital

Saying Where It Hurts

The pattern is simple: [body part] + ооруп жатат = [body part] is hurting. This progressive pattern appears in lesson 44 as well — it signals an ongoing state.

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Башым ооруп жататBashım oorup jatatMy head hurts
Курсагым ооруп жататKursagım oorup jatatMy stomach hurts
Жүрөгүм ооруп жататJürögüm oorup jatatMy heart hurts / chest pain
Буутум сындыBuutum sındıMy leg/arm is broken
Кан кетип жататKan ketip jatatI am bleeding
Ысытмам барIsıtmam barI have a fever

Instructions You Might Receive

A first responder or doctor may give you these commands. Recognising them could matter.

KyrgyzEnglish
ЖатыңызLie down
КыймылдабаңызDon't move
Дем алыңызBreathe
Бат барабызWe're going quickly

This lesson is about survival

You don't need to be fluent — you need to be understood fast. Learn these phrases until they are automatic. In a real emergency, your Kyrgyz will be understood and appreciated far more than English. The pattern [body part] + ооруп жатат will communicate the essential information clearly to any first responder.
