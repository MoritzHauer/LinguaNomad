Lesson 0003

Vowel Harmony — The Four Pairs

Every suffix in Kyrgyz echoes the vowel class of the word it attaches to. Learn the eight vowels and the four back/front pairs that govern all of Kyrgyz grammar.

Mission: travel Mission: family Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0001 — Standard Cyrillic Drill and Lesson 0002 — Kyrgyz Alphabet First Steps

Back Vowels and Front Vowels

Kyrgyz has eight vowels arranged in four pairs. Each pair has one back vowel — made with the tongue pulled back in the mouth — and one front vowel — made with the tongue pushed forward. The harmony rule is simple: back-vowel words take back-vowel suffixes; front-vowel words take front-vowel suffixes. You will see this applied in every grammar lesson from here on.

Back vowel Front vowel Back example Front example
А а Э э мал — livestock эр — husband / man
Ы ы И и кыз — girl / daughter иш — work
О о Ө ө жол — road өт — pass through
У у Ү ү уул — son үй — house / home

Back vowels: А, Ы, О, У — tongue back. Front vowels: Э, И, Ө, Ү — tongue forward.

Why It Matters For Your Mission

Suffix harmony will determine how every case ending sounds. When you see ДА vs ДЭ, or ГА vs ГЭ in later lessons, that variation is this rule in action. Getting the four pairs now means no surprises when suffixes change their vowel to match the word they attach to.

Back Root: уул (son)

The word уул contains the back vowel У. The plural suffix therefore takes its back-vowel form: -лар.

уул + лар → уулдар

уулдар = sons. The д is a linking sound that appears when the root ends in л.

Front Root: үй (house)

The word үй contains the front vowel Ү. The plural suffix therefore takes its front-rounded form: -лөр.

үй + лөр → үйлөр

үйлөр = houses. The suffix vowel Ө matches the front-rounded class of Ү in the root.
