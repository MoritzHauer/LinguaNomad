Cultural Notes

  • Чай is central to Kyrgyz hospitality — refusing tea can be seen as rude. Always accept the first cup.
  • Айран is ubiquitous — a fermented drink made from cow, sheep, or goat milk. Expect it at almost every table.
  • Бешбармак means "five fingers" — the national dish of noodles, boiled meat, and broth, eaten traditionally by hand without cutlery.
  • Нан is sacred. Bread is never thrown away; place it on the table face-up.
