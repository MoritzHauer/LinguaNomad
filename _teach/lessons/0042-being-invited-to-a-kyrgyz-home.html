Lesson 0042

Being Invited to a Kyrgyz Home

Accept or decline an invitation, respond correctly to a welcome, and use the hospitality phrases a host expects.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 20–25 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0041 — Expressing Gratitude

Arrival: Coming Through the Door

The first moments at a Kyrgyz home set the tone. Your host will guide you — use these phrases to respond gracefully.

Kyrgyz Transliteration English
Кириңиз Kiriniz Please come in
Олтуруңуз Olturunuz Please sit down
Ырахмат, кириңиз Yrahmat, kiriniz Thank you, please come in
Башыңызды ала кириңиз Bashınızdı ala kiriniz Please bring yourself in (warm welcome idiom)

At the Table

Food and drink are central to Kyrgyz hospitality. These phrases will recur every time you visit.

Kyrgyz Transliteration English
Тамак жейсизби? Tamak jeysizbı? Will you eat?
Чай ичесизби? Chay ichesizbı? Will you have tea?
Алыңыз, алыңыз Alınız, alınız Please take some, please take some
Ырахмат, болот Yrahmat, bolot Yes please / Thank you, I will
Ырахмат, жетет Yrahmat, jetet No thank you, I've had enough
Кымыз ичесизби? Kımız ichesizbı? Will you have kymyz?

Six Customs to Know Before You Arrive

  1. Remove shoes at the door — always. You will see the shoes lined up outside. Follow suit without waiting to be asked.
  2. Sit where indicated. Do not take the head of the table without an invitation — that is the elder's or host's seat.
  3. Accept tea when offered. Refusing the first offer is polite modesty. By the second offer, accept.
  4. The host fills your cup. Do not pour your own tea — that is the host's role and a sign of care.
  5. Kymyz is an honour. If fermented mare's milk is offered, accept at least a small sip respectfully. Refusing outright can seem ungracious.
  6. The most respected guest is served first. Do not start eating before the elder or guest of honour.

Leaving Well

Guest says:

Конок кылдыңыз, чоң рахмат.
Konok kıldınız, chong rahmat.
Thank you very much for hosting me.

Host says:

Жолуңуз болсун.
Jolunuz bolsun.
Have a safe journey. / May your road be good.

Hospitality is not optional — it is sacred

Kyrgyz hospitality (конокжайлык) is a core cultural value. A host who does not offer food and tea is considered to have failed their duty. As a guest, your role is to receive this warmly and not to resist it with excessive refusals. The polite dance — one soft decline, then acceptance — signals that you are not greedy, but you respect the host enough to accept what they offer. Enjoy it: you will eat well.
