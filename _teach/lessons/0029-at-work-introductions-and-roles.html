At Work — Introductions & Roles

Introduce your job and workplace, ask someone what they do for a living, and recognise the common role titles you will hear in offices and institutions.

БОЛУП ИШТЕЙМ = Work As

The key role-marker phrase in Kyrgyz is БОЛУП ИШТЕЙМ — literally "being, I work". БОЛУП is the converb of БОЛУУ (to be). It marks what role you hold:

Мен мугалим болуп иштейм. Мен дарыгер болуп иштейм.

Think of it as: МЕН + [profession noun] + БОЛУП ИШТЕЙМ. The noun comes before the marker.

Telling Someone Your Role

Use the БОЛУП ИШТЕЙМ pattern to state your profession:

  • Мен инженер болуп иштейм. — I work as an engineer.
  • Мен студентмин. — I am a student. (alternative pattern)
  • Мен мектепте иштейм. — I work at a school.

For students, СТУДЕНТМИН (I am a student) is more natural than the БОЛУП ИШТЕЙМ pattern, since studying is not paid work.

Asking Someone Else

Two standard questions cover the most ground:

  • Сиздин кесибиңиз эмне? — What is your profession? (formal)
  • Сиз кайда иштейсиз? — Where do you work? (formal)

Both use the formal second-person pronoun СИЗ. With a friend or someone younger, replace СИЗ with СЕН and adjust the verb ending.
