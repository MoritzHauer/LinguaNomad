Lesson 0046

Polite Imperative Forms

You will be able to form a polite request or instruction for any Kyrgyz verb and use the negative form correctly.

Mission: communication Skill: grammar Time: 25–30 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0034 — Pronouns & Possessive Suffixes, Lesson 0039 — Asking for Permission

The Polite Imperative Pattern

Kyrgyz forms polite imperatives by attaching a suffix to the bare verb stem. The suffix follows vowel harmony:

Vowel typeSingular politePlural polite
Back vowels (а, о, у, ы)-ыңыз-ыңыздар
Front vowels (э, ө, ü, и)-иңиз-иңиздер

Polite Imperative Examples

Verb stemPolite singularPolite pluralEnglish
кир- (enter)КириңизКириңиздерPlease come in
олтур- (sit)ОлтуруңузОлтуруңуздарPlease sit down
бер- (give)БериңизБериңиздерPlease give
айт- (say/tell)АйтыңызАйтыңыздарPlease say/tell
жаз- (write)ЖазыңызЖазыңыздарPlease write
окуй- (read)ОкуңузОкуңуздарPlease read
сүйлө- (speak)СүйлөңүзСүйлөңүздөрPlease speak

Negative Polite Imperative

To say "please don't", use a different suffix before the politeness marker:

Vowel typePatternExampleEnglish
Back vowelsstem + -баңызКирбеңизPlease don't come in
Front vowelsstem + -беңизСүйлөбөңүзPlease don't speak

Note: кир- has a front vowel (и), so the negative is Кир-беңиз, not Кир-баңыз.

Simple vs Polite Imperative

The simple imperative is just the bare verb stem, used only with peers, younger people, or close family:

RegisterFormExampleUse with
Politestem + -ыңыз/-иңизКириңизelders, strangers, formal settings
Simplebare stemКир!peers, close friends, children

The polite imperative is not optional

In Kyrgyz, the polite imperative is the default register for any public interaction — with anyone older, any stranger, or in any professional setting. Using the bare stem with an elder is a significant social error. The suffix -ыңыз/-иңиз encodes the same respect as the pronoun сиз itself. When in doubt, use the polite form.
