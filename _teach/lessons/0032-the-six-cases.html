The Six Cases — Map and Drills

Choose the right case suffix for direction, location, possession, and separation. Six cases, one mnemonic, and a full declension of ҮЙ to drill them all at once.

Mission: grammar Skill: analysis Time: 20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0031 — Vowel Harmony · Lesson 0030 — Agglutination

The Six Cases

Case Function Suffix family
NominativeSubject — who/what does the action(no suffix)
GenitivePossession — of-нын/-нин/-дын/-дин/-тын/-тин
DativeDirection / indirect object — to / for-га/-ге/-ка/-ке
AccusativeDirect object — the thing acted on-ны/-ни/-ды/-ди/-ты/-ти
LocativeLocation — in / at / on-да/-де/-та/-те
AblativeOrigin / separation — from-дан/-ден/-тан/-тен

Full Declension of ҮЙ (house)

Case Form English gloss
Nominativeүйthe house (subject)
Genitiveүйдүнof the house
Dativeүйгөto the house
Accusativeүйдүthe house (object)
Locativeүйдөin the house
Ablativeүйдөнfrom the house

Mnemonic: "No Going Doing Any Loud Arguing"

The first letters give you the order: Nominative → Genitive → Dative → Accusative → Locative → Ablative.

Say it once before you start the quiz: "No Going Doing Any Loud Arguing."
