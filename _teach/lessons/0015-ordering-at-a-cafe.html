Lesson 0015

Ordering at a Café or Чайхана

Place a food or drink order, ask the price, and leave the table gracefully — all with one sentence template.

Mission: travel Skill: ordering food Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0014 — Food & Drink Vocabulary; Lesson 0011 — Numbers 1-20

The Ordering Template: МАГА … БЕРИҢИЗ

Мага … бериңиз (Maga … beriñiz) — Give me … / I'd like …

Drop your item into the gap. No conjugation needed — the template works for everything on the menu.

Kyrgyz Transliteration English
Мага чай бериңизMaga chay beriñizGive me tea please
Мага нан бериңизMaga nan beriñizGive me bread please
Мага суу бериңизMaga suu beriñizGive me water please
Мага бешбармак бериңизMaga beshbarmak beriñizGive me beshbarmak please

Grammar note: МАГА = "to me" — the dative case of МЕН (I). The suffix -ГА/-ГЕ marks the indirect object. МЕН + ГА contracts irregularly to МАГА.

Asking the Price: КАНЧА ТУРАТ?

Канча турат? (Kancha turat?) — How much does it cost?

The answer is always … сом турат — It costs … som.

A Word About Чайхана

A чайхана (chaykhana) is a traditional tea house — part café, part community meeting point. You sit on low platforms covered with cushions, often around a low table called a takhta.

Чай arrives continuously without asking. Пиала (piala) — the small handleless bowl — is refilled by your host. When you have had enough, turn your pиала upside down on the table as a polite signal.

Food is shared rather than individually plated. Order for the table, not just yourself.
