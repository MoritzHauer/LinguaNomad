Lesson 0007

Introducing Yourself

Three building blocks — your name, where you're from, and what you do — give you a complete self-introduction in Kyrgyz.

Mission: family Mission: travel Time: 10 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0005 and Lesson 0006

Block 1 — Your Name

Template: Менин атым … (Menin atym …) — My name is …

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Менин атым Айша.Menin atym Aisha.My name is Aisha.
Менин атым Джон.Menin atym Jon.My name is John.

To ask someone's name formally: Сиздин атыңыз ким? (Sizdin atynyz kim?) — What is your name?
Informally: Сенин атың ким? (Senin atyn kim?)

Block 2 — Where You're From

Template: Мен [place] + -дан/-ден (Men [place] + -dan/-den) — I am from [place]

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Мен Бишкектенмин.Men Bishkektenmin.I am from Bishkek.
Мен Англиядан келдим.Men Angliyadan keldim.I came from England.

To ask formally: Сиз кайдандансыз? (Siz kaydandansyz?) — Where are you from?

Block 3 — What You Do

Template: Мен [profession] + -мын/-мин (Men [profession] + -myn/-min) — I am a [profession]

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Мен мугалиммин.Men mugalimmin.I am a teacher.
Мен дарыгермин.Men darygermin.I am a doctor.
Мен студентмин.Men studentmin.I am a student.

The МЕН + [noun] + МЫН/МИН Pattern

This is the simplest identity pattern in Kyrgyz. You put yourself first (МЕН = I), add the noun, then attach the copula suffix that matches the vowel:

  • After a consonant in a back-vowel word: -мын
  • After a consonant in a front-vowel word: -мин

Result: МЕН ДАРЫГЕРМИН = I am a doctor. МЕН СТУДЕНТМИН = I am a student.

A Full Introduction

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Саламатсызбы? Salamatsizdby? How do you do?
Менин атым Айша. Menin atym Aisha. My name is Aisha.
Мен Бишкектенмин. Men Bishkektenmin. I am from Bishkek.
Мен мугалиммин. Men mugalimmin. I am a teacher.

Replace Айша, Бишкек, and мугалим with your own name, city, and occupation. The grammar frames stay the same.
