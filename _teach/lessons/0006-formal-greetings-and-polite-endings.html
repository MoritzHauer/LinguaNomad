Lesson 0006

Formal Greetings & Polite Endings

Respect in Kyrgyz is built into the grammar — learn the suffix that does it and you'll navigate elders and strangers with confidence.

Mission: family Mission: travel Time: 10 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0005 — Saying Hello & How Are You

The Formal Greeting Exchange

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Саламатсызбы? Salamatsizdby? Are you well? (formal greeting)
Саламатчылык Salamatchylyk I am well. (formal response)
Сиз кандайсыз? Siz kandaysyz? How are you? (explicit formal)
Жакшы, рахмат. Сиз кандайсыз? Zhakshy, rakhmat. Siz kandaysyz? Fine, thanks. And you (formal)?

Саламатсызбы is the go-to formal opener — more respectful than Салам when meeting elders or people you've just been introduced to. Саламатчылык as a response signals that you know the register.

The -СЫЗ / -СИЗ Politeness Suffix

This suffix comes from the formal pronoun СИЗ (you, formal). When it attaches to a verb or adjective, it makes the expression respectful. Vowel harmony controls which form to use:

  • Back-vowel words take -сыз
  • Front-vowel words take -сиз
Kyrgyz Breakdown English
Саламатсызбысаламат + сыз + быAre you well?
Кандайсызкандай + сызHow are you?
Отурусузотур + усузPlease sit down (polite)

Formal Register

Саламатсызбы?Are you well?
Сиз кандайсыз?How are you?
СаламатчылыкI am well.
Жакшы, рахмат.Fine, thanks.

Informal Register

Салам!Hi!
Кандайсың?How are you? (to a friend)
Жакшы!Good!
Иштер кандай?How are things?

When to Use the Formal Register

  1. With any adult you have just met
  2. With grandparents, aunts, uncles, and parents-in-law
  3. In shops, on transport, and in official settings
  4. Whenever you are unsure — formal is never rude
