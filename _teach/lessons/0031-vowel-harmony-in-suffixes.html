Lesson 0031

Vowel Harmony in Suffixes

Every Kyrgyz suffix has multiple vowel forms. Learn the rule once and you can predict the correct form of any case ending or plural suffix — no memorisation of individual forms needed.

Mission: grammar Skill: pronunciation Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0030 — Agglutination and vowel pairs from lesson 0003

Quick Recap: The Four Vowel Classes

Kyrgyz vowels fall into four groups based on two features: front vs. back (where in the mouth they are made) and rounded vs. unrounded (whether the lips are rounded).

Class Vowels Examples
Back, unroundedа, ыбала, кыз
Back, roundedо, ужол, суу
Front, unroundedэ, иэт, иш
Front, roundedө, үөй, үй

The rule: the suffix vowel must match the class of the last vowel in the root.

The Locative Suffix in All Four Forms

Root vowel class After vowel / voiced C After voiceless C
а, ы (back, unrounded)-да-та
о, у (back, rounded)-до-то
э, и (front, unrounded)-де-те
ө, ү (front, rounded)-дө-тө

The voiced/voiceless distinction: use -д- after a vowel or voiced consonant (б, г, д, ж, з, л, м, н, ң, р); use -т- after a voiceless consonant (к, п, с, т, ф, х, ч, ш).
