Lesson 0013

Telling the Time & Days

Ask what time it is, name the day, and talk about today and tomorrow — essential scaffolding for travel and daily life.

Mission: travel Mission: family Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0011 — Numbers 1–20 · Lesson 0012 — Numbers 21–100 & Ordinals

СААТ КАНЧА? — What Time Is It?

Саат means hour or clock. Канча means how many. Together: "How many hours?" — the natural way to ask the time in Kyrgyz. State the hour number directly after саат to give the time.

Kyrgyz Transliteration English
Саат канча?Saat kancha?What time is it?
Саат бир.Saat bir.It is one o'clock.
Саат эки.Saat eki.It is two o'clock.
Саат беш.Saat besh.It is five o'clock.
Саат он эки.Saat on eki.It is twelve o'clock.
Саат бешке жарым.Saat beshke zharym.Half past five.

Жарым = half. Жарым саат = half an hour. Саат бешке жарым = literally "half to five-o'clock" — half past five.

The -ШЕМБИ Pattern Makes Days Easier

Monday through Thursday and both weekend days all end in -шемби (or -шөмбү for Monday). This ending comes from Persian and is shared across Central Asian Turkic languages — once you hear it, the days snap into place as a recognisable family. The odd one out is жума (Friday), borrowed from Arabic (the same root as Jumu'ah, the Friday prayer). Five of the seven days are therefore easy to group; жума and дүйшөмбү are the two to drill separately.
