Lesson 0037

Confirming and Correcting

Confirm something is correct, gently correct a misunderstanding, and check whether you have understood someone right.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 15–20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0036 — Likes and Dislikes

Key Phrases

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Бул туура Bul tuura That's correct
Бул туура эмес Bul tuura emes That's not correct
Бул туурабы? Bul tuurabı? Is that correct?
Ооба Ooba Yes
Жок Jok No
Туура эмес, … Tuura emes, … Not quite, … (gentle correction)
Мен туура түшүндүмбү? Men tuura tüshündümbü? Did I understand correctly?

Usage Notes

Checking your own understanding

Use Бул туурабы? after paraphrasing what someone said to check you got it right. Use Мен туура түшүндүмбү? when you want confirmation that your interpretation is correct.

Gentle vs flat correction

Туура эмес, бирок… (Not quite, but…) is far softer than a plain Жок. Pairing the correction with бирок (but) lets you guide the speaker towards the right answer rather than just shutting them down.

Sample Dialogue

Learner Сиз мугалимсизби?

Are you a teacher?
Speaker Ооба, туура.

Yes, that's right.
Learner Мен туура түшүндүмбү?

Did I understand correctly?
Speaker Ооба, туура түшүндүңүз.

Yes, you understood correctly.

The warmth of Ооба

Ооба is more than a plain "yes" — it carries warmth and engagement. Stretched out as Оооба! it expresses enthusiasm or wholehearted agreement. When a Kyrgyz speaker gives you a full Оооба!, they are not just confirming facts — they are affirming the connection.
