How a Call Unfolds

A typical Kyrgyz household phone call follows this six-step script:

  1. Caller: Алло? — Hello?
  2. Answerer: Алло, ким сүйлөп жатат? — Hello, who is speaking?
  3. Caller: Мен [аты] сүйлөп жатам. [Аты] менен сүйлөсөм болобу? — This is [name]. May I speak with [Name]?
  4. Answerer: Бир мүнөт (One moment) or Ал үйдө жок (He/she is not home)
  5. Caller: Кайра чалайынбы? — Shall I call back?
  6. Both: Кош болуңуз. — Goodbye.

Memorise these six turns and you can hold a real phone call from day one in Kyrgyzstan.

Grammar spotlight: Сүйлөп жатам

Сүйлөп жатам (I am speaking) uses the progressive aspect marker жат- combined with the verb stem. It literally means "I am in the state of speaking." This pattern works for any ongoing action: барып жатам (I am going), окуп жатам (I am reading), иштеп жатам (I am working). You will hear it constantly in spoken Kyrgyz.
