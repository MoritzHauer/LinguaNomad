Lesson 0004

Reading Kyrgyz Words Aloud

From letters to words: practice decoding family and travel vocabulary at pace. The goal is fluent word-level recognition, not letter-by-letter sounding out.

Mission: travel Mission: family Time: 15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0001, Lesson 0002, and Lesson 0003

Three Rules Before You Drill

К and Г shift with vowel class

Before and after back vowels (а, ы, о, у) the letter К sounds slightly further back in the throat — closer to a uvular stop. Before front vowels (э, и, ө, ү) it moves further forward. The same shift applies to Г. As a beginner, read both as plain k and g for now — just know the shift exists so it does not surprise you.

Stress falls on the last syllable

As a default rule, stress the final syllable: а-ТА, э-ЖЕ, у-УЛ. Most everyday Kyrgyz vocabulary follows this pattern. Loanwords and names sometimes differ, but it is the safest assumption to start with.

Chunk consonant + vowel pairs

When decoding, group letters into CV units rather than reading one letter at a time: ма-ма, а-та, э-не, жол. This is the fastest route from individual letters to words.

Why This Matters For Your Mission

These fourteen words are the first family and travel vocabulary you will encounter on signs, in homes, and in markets. Being able to read them at a glance — without mentally sounding out each letter — unlocks the next layer of practical Kyrgyz.

Recognising апа on a label, жол on a road sign, or чай on a menu is an immediate, real payoff from this session.

Three Pairs to Watch

These vowel contrasts cause the most decoding errors. Each card shows the pair and a minimal-pair example from the drill list above.

Ы · И

Ы back — кыз (girl / daughter)
И front — иш (work)

О · Ө

О back — жол (road)
Ө front — өт (pass through)

У · Ү

У back — уул (son)
Ү front — үй (house)
