At the Kiosk — Small Purchases

Buy water, bread, or a phone top-up at a street kiosk. Learn the two most essential words — бар and жок — and the handful of phrases that cover every small transaction.

Using Бар and Жок

Бар (bar) and Жок (zhok) are your two most important kiosk words. They answer almost every yes/no question about availability.

Суу бар? — Бар! Нан бар? — Жок.

To ask politely, add -бы? to the end: Суу барбы? — Do you have water?

To ask for something, use the pattern: Мага [item] бар? — literally "Is there [item] for me?"

Paying and Getting Change

After you know the price, handle the payment with these short phrases:

  • Канча турат? — How much?
  • Эки сомго. — For two som.
  • Майда акча барбы? — Do you have change?
  • Чек бериңиз. — Give me the receipt.
  • Кассага. — To the cashier. (direction given by staff)

Кiosk staff will often just hold out change rather than announce the amount — watch their hands.
