Lesson 0002

Meet the Kyrgyz Alphabet

You know the standard Cyrillic base from lesson 1. Now add the four letters that make Kyrgyz unique — and build enough confidence to sound out names, signs, and family words.

Mission: travel Mission: family Time: 10-15 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0001 — Standard Cyrillic Drill

What You Need To Know

Kyrgyz in Kyrgyzstan is normally written with Cyrillic. The current alphabet has the Russian Cyrillic base plus three extra letters that matter immediately for you: ң, ө, ү.1

You already know the standard Cyrillic base. Today you scan the whole official alphabet once to see where the Kyrgyz-specific letters sit, then spend most of your effort on those four additions.

Why This Matters For Your Mission

If you can decode the alphabet, you stop depending on transliteration. That makes street signs, names, menus, and family messages much less intimidating.

Keep the alphabet reference sheet open beside this lesson.

Start With The Four Letters That Change Everything

Ң ң

ng as in sing. This is one of the three extra Kyrgyz letters.

Ө ө

A rounded front vowel. If you know German, think o-umlaut; if not, just keep it separate from О о.

Ү ү

Another rounded front vowel. Keep it separate from У у.

Ы ы

A back vowel with no clean English equivalent. Do not collapse it into И и.

Two Visual Traps

  • Р р is r, not English p.
  • С с is s, not English c.
  • У у and Ү ү are different vowels.
  • О о and Ө ө are different vowels.

Read These Without Panic

These examples come straight from standard Kyrgyz writing found in the reference sources:

кыргыз өз тең тили

You are not expected to pronounce these perfectly yet. The goal is to notice the script pieces: ы, ө, ң, and familiar letters around them.
