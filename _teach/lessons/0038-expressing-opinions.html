Lesson 0038

Expressing Opinions

Share a personal opinion, agree or disagree, and invite someone else's view in Kyrgyz.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 15–20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0037 — Confirming and Correcting

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish

Grammar: Мен ойлойм

Мен ойлойм is a complete verb phrase meaning "I think". Unlike English, Kyrgyz needs no subordinating conjunction (no equivalent of "that") — simply follow it with the thought.

KyrgyzEnglish
Мен ойлойм, ал жакшы. I think it is good.
Мен ойлойм, Кыргызстан сулуу. I think Kyrgyzstan is beautiful.
Менимче, бул оор эмес. In my opinion, this is not hard.

Менимче is a more personal frame — it foregrounds subjectivity. Use it when you want to signal that this is your personal view rather than a general claim.

Agreeing and Disagreeing

Agreement

Мен макулмун

I agree

Мен дагы ошондой ойлойм

I think so too

Disagreement

Мен макул эмесмин

I disagree

Менимче, туура эмес

In my opinion, that's not right

Sample Conversation

Learner Кандай ойлойсуз? Кыргызстан сулуубу?

What do you think? Is Kyrgyzstan beautiful?
Speaker Мен макулмун, абдан сулуу!

I agree, very beautiful!
Learner Мен дагы ошондой ойлойм.

I think so too.

Кызыктуу — a safe landing pad

Кызыктуу (interesting) is a socially safe response when you have heard an opinion but haven't formed your own yet. It acknowledges the speaker warmly without committing to agreement or disagreement — useful in cross-cultural conversations where you need a moment to think.
