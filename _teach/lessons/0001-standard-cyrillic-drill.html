Lesson 0001

Standard Cyrillic Drill

Start here: the standard Cyrillic base shared with Russian. Lock in the false friends and new shapes — the Kyrgyz-specific letters in lesson 2 build on top of these.

Mission: travel Mission: family Time: 15 minutes

Your Starting Point

This is lesson 1 — you are starting from zero. Today you build the standard Cyrillic base: false friends and new shapes. The four Kyrgyz-specific letters come in lesson 2 on top of what you learn here.

Your Win Today

Read a short Kyrgyz word letter by letter without misfiring on a false friend.

Keep the reference sheet beside you.

False Friends

These are the letters most likely to trigger the wrong sound. Your eye sees a Latin shape; you must override it.

Н н

n, not h. The two verticals look exactly like H, but the sound is n as in name.

В в

v, not b. Remember: Б б is b. В в is v as in vine.

Х х

kh, not x. A throaty fricative — the ch in German Bach or Scottish loch.

У у

u as in moon, not y. Looks like a tilted Y, but it is a pure back vowel.

И и

i as in see, not a reversed N. Front vowel — keep it separate from ы (lesson 2).

Р р

r, not p. Looks exactly like the Latin letter P, but the sound is r as in run.

С с

s, not c. Looks like the Latin letter C, but the sound is s as in sun.

Lucky Matches

These look similar to Latin letters and carry roughly the same sound. Enjoy the relief.

А а

Open a vowel. Same shape, same basic sound as Latin A.

О о

Back rounded o. Familiar shape, familiar sound.

М м

m. Looks just like Latin M, sounds just like m.

Т т

t. Looks like a Latin T with a flat crossbar.

К к

k. Recognizable shape, predictable sound.

Е е

e or ye at word starts. Close enough to English E to be useful.

New Shapes

No Latin equivalent. The mnemonics below are memory hooks only — not sound descriptions. Learn the hook, then let it go once the letter sticks.

Б б

b. Think of the number 6 rotated — the bump faces right. Like ball.

Г г

g. Looks like a reversed L — a right-angle. Like the start of go.

Д д

d. A tent or arch shape with legs. Like the start of dog.

Ж ж

zh. The middle consonant in measure. Looks like a snowflake or beetle.

З з

z. Looks like the number 3. Straightforward — just z.

Й й

y as in yes. Like И but with a small arch (breve) on top.

Л л

l. A wedge — think of the Greek lambda λ. Like lamp.

П п

p. Looks like a doorway or archway. Not r — that is р (lesson 1).

Ш ш

sh. Three downward teeth — like the bottom of a comb. Like ship.

Ч ч

ch. Looks like a small cup or bowl on its side. Like cherry.

Try Reading These

Sound out each word using the letters you now know. Slow is fine. The goal is letters, not meaning.

баш жол там банк

баш = head / start · жол = road / path · там = wall · банк = bank (loanword)
