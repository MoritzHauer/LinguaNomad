Lesson 0008

Taking Leave

End every conversation with grace — Kyrgyz goodbyes are warm and specific. The right farewell depends on who is leaving and what time of day it is.

Mission: family Mission: travel Time: 10 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0005, 0006, 0007

Standard Goodbyes

Kyrgyz Sounds like English Register
Кош болуңуз Kosh bolunuz Goodbye Formal — to person staying
Кош бол Kosh bol Bye Informal — to person staying
Жолуңуз болсун Zholunuz bolsun Safe travels To person leaving — very common

Жолуңуз болсун — the Kyrgyz farewell blessing

This phrase literally means "may your road be good" — жол (road/journey) + -уңуз (your, formal) + болсун (let it be). Say it to anyone who is leaving for a trip, even a short one. You will hear it constantly, and saying it back earns immediate warmth. It is not just formal — it is deeply human.

See-You Phrases

Kyrgyz Sounds like English
Кездешебиз Kezdesheibiz We'll meet again / See you
Дагы кездешебиз Dagy kezdesheibiz See you again
Эртең көрүшөбүз Erten körüshöbüz See you tomorrow
Чао Chao Bye (colloquial)
Ийги! Iygi! Great! / All good! (farewell acknowledgement)

Night Farewells

Kyrgyz Sounds like English Register
Жакшы жатыңыз Zhakshy zhatynyz Good night Formal — literally "sleep well"
Жакшы жат Zhakshy zhat Good night Informal

The -ЫҢЫЗ/-ИҢИЗ ending you see in ЖАТЫҢЫЗ is the same formal possessive suffix from lesson 6 — it attaches to verbs here to make the command polite.
