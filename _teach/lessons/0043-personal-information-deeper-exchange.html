Lesson 0043

Personal Information: Deeper Exchange

Ask and answer a fuller set of personal questions — age, hometown, occupation, family — and handle follow-up questions gracefully.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 20–25 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0042 — Being Invited to a Kyrgyz Home

Questions and Answer Patterns

These six questions form the backbone of a first real conversation in Kyrgyzstan. Learn both the question and the answer frame.

Grammar: Locative vs. Ablative Suffix

Two suffixes do a lot of work in these personal questions. They follow vowel harmony — the vowel in the suffix matches the last vowel of the stem.

-да / -де / -та / -те — Locative: in / at

Бишкекте жашайм
Bishkekte jashaym
I live in Bishkek.

Үйдө турам
Üydö turam
I stay at home.

-дан / -ден / -тан / -тен — Ablative: from

Бишкектен болом
Bishkekten bolom
I am from Bishkek.

Оштон болом
Oşton bolom
I am from Osh.

Personal questions are warm, not intrusive

In Kyrgyzstan, asking about someone's age, marital status, and occupation at a first meeting is completely normal — it shows interest and begins building a social picture. Asking about family is especially well received: "Балдарыңыз барбы?" (Do you have children?) is almost always heard as a sign of genuine connection, not nosiness. Lean in, answer openly, and ask back.
