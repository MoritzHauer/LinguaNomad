Lesson 0035

Clarification: "I Didn't Understand"

Deploy three recovery strategies — signal confusion, request repetition, and ask for slower speech — to stay in any Kyrgyz conversation.

Mission: communication Skill: conversation Time: 15–20 minutes

Builds on: Lesson 0034 — Pronouns & Possessive Suffixes

The Three Recovery Strategies

When comprehension fails you have three tools: signal confusion, request repetition, or ask for slower speech. In sequence, they let you recover from any breakdown without losing the conversation.

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Кечиресиз Kechireсiz Excuse me / Sorry
Мен түшүнгөн жокмун Men tüshüngön jokmun I didn't understand
Кайталаңызчы Kaytalañızchı Please repeat that
Жайыраак сүйлөңүзчү Jayıraak süylöñüzchü Please speak more slowly

Strategy 1: signal → Strategy 2: repeat → Strategy 3: slow down. Use кечиресиз before any of them.

Finding a Shared Language

If the three strategies do not help, checking for a shared language is a valid fallback.

KyrgyzTransliterationEnglish
Кыргызча билесизби? Kyrgyzcha bilesizbi? Do you speak Kyrgyz?
Англисче билесизби? Anglisсhe bilesizbi? Do you speak English?
Жок, билбейм Jok, bilbeym No, I don't know

The pattern -ча/-че билесизби? works with any language name: Орусча (Russian), Француздча (French), etc.

Sample Dialogue

Speaker:

Эртең базарга барасыз беле?

Are you going to the bazaar tomorrow?

Learner:

Кечиресиз, мен түшүнгөн жокмун. Жайыраак сүйлөңүзчү.

Sorry, I didn't understand. Please speak more slowly.

Cultural note: Honesty is Respect

In Kyrgyz culture it is always better to say you didn't understand than to nod along pretending you did. Elders especially appreciate honesty. Always lead with кечиресиз (excuse me) before any clarification request — it softens the interruption and signals respect.
